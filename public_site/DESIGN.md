# starBoard public site design brief

## Art direction

- Goal: Make the public `www.fhl-star-board.com` page feel like a living sunflower sea star recovery and field-identification project, not a generic dark SaaS launch page.
- Primary users: field collaborators, reviewers, UW/FHL-adjacent researchers, supporters/donors, and technically curious visitors.
- Core action: route collaborators to the field portal or browser workspace while giving public visitors enough context to understand and trust the project.
- Desired feeling: field-science, Salish Sea, careful recovery monitoring, practical research tooling, in active development.
- Visual style: dark kelp/ocean base, warm sea-star accent, field notebook/data-overlay details, restrained scientific UI mockups.
- References / traits to borrow: warm editorial hierarchy and restrained borders from Notion-like layouts; dense-but-legible metadata from scientific field records; compact developer-tool status chips where useful.
- Anti-references: generic purple/blue SaaS gradients, empty glass cards, fake dashboard hero art, vague startup copy, equal-weight CTA clouds.
- Brand constraints: keep `starBoard`, public contact `wlweert@gmail.com`, active-development language, and existing public links.
- Content/assets: no committed public sea star photo asset yet, so use CSS-generated specimen/data visual rather than publishing private archive photos.
- Device priorities: public desktop and phone views must be readable; collaborator portal links should remain obvious.
- Accessibility: strong contrast, visible focus states, semantic HTML, no hover-only critical actions.

## Success criteria

- The first viewport communicates sunflower sea star recovery, field photos, individual histories, and protected collaborator tools.
- CTAs are grouped by audience instead of six equal pills.
- Visual identity uses species/project-specific cues: field record metadata, candidate match preview, Salish Sea/recovery language, and sea-star-inspired accent color.
- No claim of visual completion without local browser screenshots and console/build checks.
