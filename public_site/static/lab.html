UW Friday Harbor Laboratories sea star work

starBoard supports practical monitoring and review workflows connected to sunflower sea star recovery work in the Salish Sea.

Friday Harbor research context

Dr. Jason Hodin is a Senior Research Scientist at UW Friday Harbor Laboratories. His sea star rearing lab has been raising sunflower sea stars in captivity since 2019, with the long-term goal of supporting recovery of healthy sea stars in the wild.

Why sunflower stars matter

Sunflower sea stars (Pycnopodia helianthoides) are important predators in Northeast Pacific kelp forest ecosystems. After wasting disease drove severe declines, purple sea urchins expanded in many places and contributed to overgrazing pressure on kelp forests.

How starBoard fits

starBoard is an in-development field-to-analysis tool for organizing observations, photos, locations, and candidate visual matches so individual sea stars can be reviewed consistently over time.

Background summarized from UW Friday Harbor Laboratories and UW College of the Environment public materials about Dr. Jason Hodin's sunflower sea star rearing work. To support sea star recovery, visit the UW Together campaign.
